Educators Teach Self Advocacy

Move over, TED Talks—Here's something even better. 

By Kristina Buck
Meet Molly, a middle school student who is deafblind and known around campus for her happy heart, kind soul, and contagious laugh. She has a way of bringing light wherever she goes—and during her most recent Individualized Education Plan (IEP) meeting, she did exactly that. Molly took center stage, leading an engaging "All About Me" presentation that filled the room with smiles. With Rachel's (her intervener) support, she confidently shared her favorite things, articulated the accommodations and supports that empowered her the most, and sprinkled in fun facts that showcased her vibrant personality. This presentation wasn't just a heartwarming moment but a significant stride toward one of Molly's Expanded Core Curriculum (ECC) goals: self-determination. By advocating for herself and fostering a deeper understanding of her needs within her team, Molly embraced ownership of her learning in an inspiring and profoundly empowering manner.
 
Way to go, Molly—you didn't just attend your IEP, you made it shine!  
Enrichment 
Utah School for the Deaf 

What an amazing adventure the deaf students had on a trip to Boston in April. Our students learned so much about history, and truly enjoyed every moment — it was a trip to remember! They researched and visited the Boston Tea Party Ship, Freedom Trail, Paul Revere's House, Bunker Hill Monument, Fenway Park, the Mayflower, and Martha's Vineyard to highlight a few. We’re so proud of our students for soaking in every experience and deepening their understanding of our nation’s history. 

Factoid: Travel learning opportunities are not funded by education budgets or taxpayer dollars. These activities are paid for with separate trust funds that were set aside specifically to enrich the lives of Utah children who are deaf or blind, years ago, when Utah attained statehood.

Making the World a Better Place
 

Teachers at the Utah Schools for the Deaf and Blind are celebrating their own graduation! Several educators from the Blind and Deafblind Outreach Programs have completed the University of Utah’s Visual Impairment Master’s program. A few of them gaining an endorsement in deafblind services. These dedicated professionals (along with a few others not pictured) are now certified Teachers of Students with Visual Impairments (TVIs).

A TVI is a specialized educator who supports students who are blind or visually impaired in accessing education and developing essential life skills. TVIs teach academic content, Braille, assistive technology, and functional skills.

A teacher of students who have deafblindness is specially trained to support children who have some degree of both blindness and deafness. In addition to studying deafblindness, they receive training in areas like American Sign Language.
