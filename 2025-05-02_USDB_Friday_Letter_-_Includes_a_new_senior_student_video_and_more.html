Celebrating a Baby Chick
Utah School for the Blind Preschool
Preschool Teacher, Maria Lira Jukic and her students are celebrating the hatching of a baby chick. The teacher brought an incubator into her classroom at the Utah School for the Blind on the Ogden campus. The students have been watching its progress over the past few weeks. Finally, the baby chick broke its way out of the egg shell!

Karen Borg, director of the Early Childhood Division at the Utah School for the Blind said, “This was such a great opportunity for the children to care for life and to anticipate over time. Utah School for the Deaf
 Modeling Erosion and Preventing Flooding
Elizabeth DeLong School of the Deaf students celebrated National Park Week with an experiential learning opportunity led by Educational Ranger Hailey at Timpanogos Cave National Monument. During a hike along the rushing river, the middle school students explored how weathering and erosion happens in the park. Hands-on activities were interspersed throughout the visit. Teacher of the Deaf, Deanna Glasser, said, "One activity was a relay game modeling the erosion and deposition process. Another was testing ways to prevent flooding. We were proud to honor the significance of the week by learning about the preservation of our national heritage, connecting with nature and exploring the great outdoors."

Southern Utah School of the Deaf
Snow Canyon State Park
Photography by USDB Intern, Madi Anderson

Southern Utah School of the Deaf’s high school and middle school classes went on a field trip to Snow Canyon State Park last week. Teacher Natalie Graham shared, "We enjoyed Miss Cheyenne, a park naturalist, leading our hike. We were a small but mighty group that headed out into the wilderness on a one hour and one mile hike. We have been learning about erosion and how it affects us down here in Southern Utah. We were able to see arches and walk through slot canyons. Miss Cheyenne was able to show us zombie moss. Zombie moss goes dormant during the hot months and when it rains it comes back to life. She poured water on the moss and it turned bright green. Thank you to the state of Utah for giving us complimentary park passes and making this trip possible." More Photos
The Hope Squad

The Annual Hope Squad Retreat brought together Hope Squad students from Jean Massieu School and Kenneth Burdett School at the USDB Ogden campus. Activities included fun challenges like carrying M&Ms with straws, a three-legged race with handkerchiefs, popping balloons by stomping, making s'mores around a campfire, tie-dyeing shirts, and playing a variety of board and table games. On Saturday morning, students participated in a group discussion about their Hope Squad programs. Advisors included Whitney James, Jannon Cornwall, Wade Hester, and Kim Thornsberry. CDI Heather Aldridge provided interpreting services. It was a successful retreat, filled with laughter and smiles from all the students.

Wearable Tech for the Blind
By Tammy Noble, Orientation and Mobility Specialist
David Muamua Gaoa, a post-high school student from Hurricane, had the exciting opportunity to try Meta Ray-Ban smart glasses for the first time last week, and his reaction was nothing short of joyful. He eagerly took a selfie wearing the glasses, and it was clear he was amazed by the technology. The glasses were connected to an iPhone. The highlight of the experience came when he looked at a vending machine through the glasses and was able to identify his favorite soda—root beer—along with the correct vendor number to buy the drink, all using the meta glasses. It was a great example of how assistive technology can support independence and engagement for students transitioning into adulthood. His excitement was contagious, and the experience sparked great conversations among students and staff about the future of wearable tech.
Welcome Rayson and Preslee!

USDB is excited to welcome Rayson, a new student receiving deafblind services, and his intervener, Preslee. Preslee is already helping Rayson explore the world in new ways—he is experiencing going down the slide for the first time and is learning to use his white cane to navigate with more confidence. We’re thrilled to have them on the team and can’t wait to see what they accomplish together!

Mia, will be turning 3 soon and the family wanted to come tour her preschool at Jean Massieu School.
